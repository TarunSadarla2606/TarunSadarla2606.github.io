About

to a research career.

I pursued my MS at the University of North Texas specifically to build the depth needed to do this work properly. Research Interests

What I'm thinking about

Problems I find genuinely interesting and want to work on next.

01
Explainable AI in Radiology
Clinicians need to understand why a model made a prediction. Grad-CAM, SHAP, and attention visualization as tools for clinical trust — not just academic reporting.
02
Low-Data Medical Imaging
Clinical datasets are small, imbalanced, and expensive to annotate. Few-shot learning, self-supervised pretraining, and synthetic augmentation for radiology and pathology.
03
Clinical Deployment Constraints
A model that achieves 97% on a benchmark means nothing if it can't run in a hospital. Model compression, quantization, and real-time inference for imaging pipelines.
04
Temporal Imaging Analysis
Static frames lose information. Cine-loop ultrasound, longitudinal MRI, and time-series signal data reveal dynamics that single-image models miss entirely.
